# Week 7 Results Tables

## Figures Generated

The following figures have been generated from the current 41 completed sessions:

1. `analysis/charts/pre-post-awareness.svg`
   - Shows the mean pre-test score and mean post-test score.
   - Main point: the post-test score is lower, which is interpreted as self-perception recalibration.

2. `analysis/charts/gap-indicators.svg`
   - Shows how many participants had each type of self-perception/choice gap.
   - Main point: 31 out of 41 participants had at least one gap.

3. `analysis/charts/mismatch-recalibration.svg`
   - Shows the relationship between mismatch count and recalibration.
   - Main point: participants with more mismatches tended to lower their post-test self-rating more.

4. `analysis/charts/action-distribution.svg`
   - Shows selected action frequencies across the shopping stages.
   - Main point: this is useful as an optional presentation figure if the audience needs to see what participants actually clicked.

The lower post-test score means many participants became less confident that they could predict or explain their own choices. This supports the idea of self-perception recalibration.

Main qualitative pattern:

Participants often mentioned page details, default services, reviews, price pressure, and time pressure. Some directly said that they originally thought they would act carefully, but their actual simulated choices were faster or less careful than expected.

Representative anonymous responses:

1. "I thought I knew this kind of promotion trick, but I still clicked it. The main reason was that it happened to be something I had recently been looking at."
2. "I thought I would compare more, but after seeing the price drop I quickly moved into checkout."
3. "I was very confident in my own judgment, but after adding something to the cart, I was more likely to continue along the process."
